/HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[1]/text()[2] i am the orchestra director at meadowood baptist church in midwest city, oklahoma. i have been in music ministry for over ten years. my wife, dawn, and i have been married for over ten years and have two incredible children, jayden (3) and abigail (1). love has come is my first writing project. it was born out a ministry ordeal that, at one point, i was not sure i would be able to heal from. my short tenure in ministry has produced many incredible experiences, but it has also produced emotional setbacks and discouragement. as i started to reflect back on my ministry to date, i realized god has remained faithful. this book highlights a few of those ministry experiences in order to reveal god's abundant grace and peace. /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[2]/B[1]/text()[1] what was your motivation behind this project? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[2]/text()[2] my aspiration is that love has come will be a resource that helps others find hope and rediscover the greatness of jesus. i found solace and hope from committing to a disciplined, daily quiet time while i was undergoing a painful ordeal. through my study of matthew, i learned jesus is love personified. i learned jesus maintained a heavenly kingdom-focused mindset in the midst of a worldly-focused creation. i learned jesus saw the worst in humanity but spoke and acted with the intention of achieving the best in humanity. i learned jesus honored god through humility, not haughtiness. i learned jesus is the way, truth, and life. i learned jesus mends a broken heart. i couldn't keep this message of encouragement to myself. my spirit compelled to put in on paper which resulted in the completion of love has come. /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[3]/B[1]/text()[1] what do you hope folks will gain from this project? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[3]/text()[2] i want to help others know that scripture is more than a historical manuscript. it?s more than a codified set of morals and beliefs. it is a love-filled text that has relevance for everyone. it is the inspired message of a gracious god. nowhere is that better exemplified than through the ministry, life, death, and resurrection of jesus. through love has come, i desire to impart a greater appreciation of jesus, god?s son and humanity?s savior, who forever changed history. /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[4]/B[1]/text()[1] how were you personally impacted by working on this project? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[4]/text()[2] i remember sitting at my kitchen table one morning staring at my bible. in the silence of the early morning hour, i kept thinking about the spiritual storm i was facing. i had just been let go as the music minister of a church i had served in for nearly three years. the events that had led up to my dismissal from the church were guarded with the utmost secrecy by the pastor, who's personality differences with me were justification for my dismissal in his eyes. negative thoughts looped through my mind. i was distraught. i was frustrated. i was upset. ultimately, i sensed i was on the cusp of a huge spiritual decision. i could either remain in a state of dissatisfaction with god and ministry, which i knew would likely lead to a lasting bitterness, or i could release my pain to god and ask him to help me handle my emotions. instinctively, i knew i needed to release my pain but i just didn?t know how to do it. i prayed god would give me direction and hope. i felt god telling me to read matthew?s gospel. apprehensively, i opened up my bible to chapter one. something remarkable happened: god?s message took on amplified meaning. i was overwhelmed with encouragement and application. this time of study and prayer became a daily habit. at the end of the biblical odyssey, i was left with with inspirational thoughts and a mending heart. matthew?s twenty-eight chapters provided me much needed encouragement and a renewed purpose. i found myself viewing the spiritual storm i was enduring within the greater framework of my life. through every ordeal god had proven himself faithful. from that context, the momentary trouble i was undergoing did not seem as difficult as i initially made it out to be. looking through the lens of my life, i saw a dedicated god who was committed not only to saving me, but guiding me, blessing me, molding me, and reassuring me. /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[5]/B[1]/text()[1] who are your influences, sources of inspiration or favorite authors / artists? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[2]/DIV[14]/P[5]/text()[2] i continue to expand my personal library of books and therefore my exposure to various writing styles, but there are three primary authors whose books have deeply inspired me and caused me to turn to their works time and time again: philip yancey, charles swindoll, and max lucado. philip yancey provides profound insight and depth into his material. he challenges readers to dig deeper into god?s word and move beyond a superficial life. his book, the jesus i never knew, is immensely powerful. reading it was a life-changing event for me. charles swindoll writes books that offer an expository review of the bible. he is a teacher that inspires with a servant?s heart. his messages bring forth conviction and encouragement. max lucado creates incredible word pictures. he has mastered the art of mental illustration and allows readers to see the word of god in a greater light. i have yet to read a book by max lucado that did not make me marvel at the beauty of god?s glorious, grand design. statement of matthew 1: love has arrived. i was convinced that should be the title for a book when, suddenly, the chorus to the rich mullins song ?hope to carry on? came to my mind (actually caedmon?s call updated version of the song is what came to my mind because i listened to their version of the song so many times in my youth). the repetitive, powerful lyrics on the chorus of ?hope to carry on? state: love has come love has come love has come and it?s given me hope to carry on i knew immediately love has come was the ideal book title because it adequately summarized matthew?s central message
