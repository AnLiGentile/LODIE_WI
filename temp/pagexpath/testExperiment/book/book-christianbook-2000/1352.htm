"step inside" is a collection of 12 stories--stories i've told for many years and stories that are freshly written. my hope is that "step inside" hangs around a bit...is used by listeners in their everyday living... that it sits on coffee tables and bedsides stands, that it's share with others, and that the cd is in the car and ready for a car ride or two. /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[13]/P[3]/B[1]/text()[1] what do you hope folks will gain from this project? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[13]/P[3]/text()[2] a moment to breathe and rest in the goodness of a story--whether it's on the page of the book or by way of the cd. i want to give a fresh insight into god's redemptive and restoring love. i invite my reader to take the risk and tell a story at home, in a school, at a bible study, in a worship moment, by the side of a bed, or in a friendly gathering. /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[13]/P[4]/B[1]/text()[1] how were you personally impacted by working on this project? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[13]/P[4]/text()[2] this book is co published with wine press and my own storytelling ministry of right-side-up stories. a book with a gorgeous cover, wonderful art, a musically scored cd, meaningful stories and beautiful packaging does not happen without a team of artists and caring production people. i became a better writer and storyteller...a better manager! /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[13]/P[5]/B[1]/text()[1] who are your influences, sources of inspiration or favorite authors / artists? /HTML[1]/BODY[1]/DIV[1]/DIV[13]/P[5]/text()[2] authors i treasure: c.s. lewis, henri nouwen, walter wangerin, lois cheney, brennan manning, "author unknown", the old storytellers never given titles or fame, but were simply true to the work of telling it out-loud or writing it down. i am moved by these artists as well... michael card, michael w. smith, dennis jernigan, darlene zschech, matt redman...alberto and kimberly rivera
